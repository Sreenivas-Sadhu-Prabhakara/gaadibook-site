The problem

A diary and a good memory don't scale past two vehicles.

Who this hurts

The owner of three tempos in Ludhiana. The cab operator running five sedans on Ola. The auto owner in Pune who is also the driver, the accountant and the dispatcher.

You don't have a fleet department. You have a phone, a plastic folder of papers, and a due date you're trying not to forget.

  • WhatsApp The renewal reminder from your agent is buried under 400 other chats by the time it matters.
  • The diary Six documents × five vehicles = 30 dates. One page, one pen, and no alarm that goes off.
  • Memory You remember insurance because it's expensive. You forget PUC and fitness — until the checkpoint.
  • The cost Driving without insurance: up to Rs 4,000 + points. An expired-permit impound can idle the vehicle for a week.
The insight

Every vehicle document is pure downside. Renewing on time gets you nothing. Missing it by one day gets you a fine or an impound. So the only job that matters is not lapsing — and that's a calendar problem, not a paperwork problem.

GaadiBook does one thing well: it makes the next lapse impossible to miss, and ranks your vehicles by what a lapse would actually cost you.

How it works

Set it up once. It watches the dates for you.

  1. 1

    Add your vehicles

    Enter each number plate and the expiry date on every document. Two minutes per vehicle, and you never touch it again unless something renews.

  2. 2

    Get a risk score

    Each vehicle is scored by how soon a document lapses and how much that specific lapse costs. The riskiest gaadi rises to the top.

  3. 3

    Reminders go out

    Staged nudges at T-30, 15, 7 and 1 day — then an expired alert — land on WhatsApp and SMS. You act while there's still time.

  4. 4

    Work the list

    Open the renew-by worklist, top to bottom, most urgent first. Renew, mark done, and the vehicle drops off the danger list.

Questions
Do I need to install an app?

No. GaadiBook works in your browser and sends reminders straight to WhatsApp and SMS. Nothing to download, nothing to update.

Does it renew the documents for me?

GaadiBook doesn't file renewals — it makes sure you never miss the window. It tracks every date, ranks by risk and nudges you in time to renew through your usual agent or the RTO.

What if I have just one vehicle?

Still worth it. Six documents on one gaadi is six chances to slip. The plan is flat at Rs 299/mo whether you track one vehicle or twenty.
